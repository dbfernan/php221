Give Blood, Save Lives

A blood donation from a single person can save up to three lives, making them a vital part of our healthcare system. Blood cannot be manufactured and its only source is from donors.

Serving Patients in Critical Need

Examples Include:

  • Accident Victims
  • Burn Victims
  • Cancer Patients
  • Surgery Patients
  • Chronic Blood Disorder Patients

Blood donations can also reduce the risk of iron overload, lower the risk of heart disease, and treat sickle cell disease.

Boosting the National Blood Supply

Access to blood transfusions is critical to healthcare because it directly impacts patient survival and recovery in various medical situations. Securing blood donations ensures that hospitals have enough blood supply throughout each procedure.

As one of the only non-profit organizations in the United States that focuses on providing blood services through voluntary donations, the American Red Cross is vital to maintaining the blood supply for our target population. In Healthy People 2030, this is critical to achieving the "Increasing Access to Healthcare Services" objective.

Join the Life-Saving Mission

The American Red Cross accepts whole blood donations from anyone:

  • Every 56 days, up to 6 times a year
  • Aged 16+
  • At Least 110 Pounds
  • Has Sufficient Iron Levels
  • Has No Other Signs of Chronic Health Conditions

For information about Power Red, Platelet, and AB Elite Plasma donations, consult the American Red Cross' Official Website

People can also apply to host their own blood drives! As a Blood Program Leader, you play a key role in raising blood donation awareness by promoting blood drives, recruiting volunteers, and educating the target population about the importance of consistent blood donations to maintain a stable and diverse blood supply.
